User Interviews

Through the user interviews I was able to really gain insight into the perspective of potential employers, friends, fellow prospects, etc. I hadn’t considered many things I had found through the user interviews. For example, the aesthetics of a website and personalization really mattered to those I interviewed. However I also should focus on content just as much.

Another important thing I learned from the user interviews is actually, how not to do them. I realize I asked many not relevant questions during the interviews that weren’t beneficial to the problem at hand. Overall, I learned to ask more specific questions, and always ask questions in between the interview process as well.

Competitor Analysis

A competitor analysis allows me to identify those competitors in the same field as me with portfolios as well. I evaluated their strategies and how certain things worked for them or didn’t. Because I didn’t have much experience building websites I also used this to help me get a grasp of how to design my own website.

I analyzed 3 different competitors.

  1. I analyzed a fellow software developer, Neil. I reviewed this portfolio because I wanted to see an example of a website of someone in my current position.

  2. I analyzed another portfolio of a fellow developer and designer. Since Kalyn was also a designer I was able to view many deign aspects through this competitive analysis and see how those came into play when tying in also their coding abilities.

  3. Lastly, I analyzed, LinkedIn. As a website that displays multiple people’s profiles, I saw LinkedIn as high competition. LinkedIn is used and trusted by many therefore I wanted to gain insight on how to convey that same trust and professionalism onto my own website.

Feature Value Matrix

The feature value matrix helps rank features of the website on varying qualities, so that all stakeholders can understand the product needs and possibilities.

  1. Contact by Email: I included this because after the user interviews and competitive analysis, it became obvious it should always be included so that potential employers can always contact me.

  2. Navigation Bar: A nav bar was necessary to help users navigate from one part of the website to another

  3. Introduction/Bio Section: It seemed imperative to include a short introductory paragraph to personalize the webpage.

  4. Photo: An image of myself humanized the website and put a face onto the person being described.

  5. Hobbies/Interests: This section was selected to be included in my website again to personalize the website and add my character. I wanted to feature parts of my life that make me who I am.

  6. Experience Section: I thought it to be extremely important to include this so that employers are aware of my capabilities. Due to time constraints I have yet to add this page but plan to.

  7. Projects/Personal Projects: I included personal projects as well as class ones also to show what i currently know how to do as well as what programs I am familiar with.

  8. Relevant social media profiles: I feel it's important to include my professional LinkedIn account to show what connections I have already and then also included my gitHub so they can view my work with current projects.

  9. Resume/Link to Resume: This was in my original plan however, from the presentations I learned that the resume's should not be linked do to information can be stolen.

  10. Visual aspects to support some information: I made sure to include visual aspects throughout my entire website to support my content and not bore the viewer.
